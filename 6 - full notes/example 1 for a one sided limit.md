# example 1 for a one sided limit

TD: YEAR1::TERM2::MA139 Analysis 2::02 Limits and the derivative
Q: Let $f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ be defined by
$$
f(x) = 
\begin{cases} 
0 & \text{if } x < 0, \\
\frac{1}{2} & \text{if } x = 0, \\
1 & \text{if } x > 0.
\end{cases}
$$
Then what are
$$
\lim_{x \to 0^+} f(x) \quad, \quad \lim_{x \to 0^-} f(x).
$$
A: $$
\lim_{x \to 0^+} f(x) = 1
\quad\quad
\lim_{x \to 0^-} f(x) = 0.
$$
