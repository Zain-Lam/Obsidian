# example 2 for limit of a function

TD: YEAR1::TERM2::MA139 Analysis 2::02 Limits and the derivative
Q: prove that $$
\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} = 2.
$$
A: As long as $x \neq 1$ then,#
$$
\frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} = x + 1,
$$
and as $x \to 1$, this approaches $2$
